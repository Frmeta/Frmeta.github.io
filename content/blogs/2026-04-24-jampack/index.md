I am incredibly excited to announce that my recent puzzle project, [Waste Crusher]( {{< ref "/project/2026-03-22-waste-crusher.md" >}} ), has officially been selected to join Jampack, a charity-focused gaming platform supporting Games For Love!

The website is up now up at [https://jampack.org/games/waste-crusher/](https://jampack.org/games/waste-crusher/). Please note that you need to buy subscription in order to play from Jampack's website.

### The Journey
Waste Crusher started as a project built "under pressure" for a game jam. The goal was to create a tactile, industrial-grade puzzle experience where players don't just find a fit—they manufacture it. Between crushing metal, slicing oversized trash, and strategically burying toxic waste, the game became a challenge of spatial logic and resource management.

When the team at Games For Love reached out, it was an easy "yes." Their mission is to bring entertainment and distraction to children undergoing medical treatment in hospitals. Knowing that the mechanics I built during a weekend of frantic coding will now help brighten the days of young patients adds a whole new layer of meaning to the project.

By joining the Jampack library, Waste Crusher will reach a brand-new audience of players who are gaming for a cause. Seeing my work live on a professional platform like Jampack is a huge milestone. Huge thanks to Granit and the GFL team for the opportunity!
